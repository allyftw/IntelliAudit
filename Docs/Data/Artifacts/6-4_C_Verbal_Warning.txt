This email serves as formal documentation of disciplinary action taken against Joseph Thomas, Systems Administrator in the IT Operations team, in accordance with our organization's Information Security Policy and ISO 27001:2022 Control 6.4 (Disciplinary Process).

INCIDENT SUMMARY:
On January 8, 2026, at approximately 10:42 AM, Mr. Thomas was observed by Security Operations Center (SOC) personnel sharing his privileged administrative credentials with a colleague, Michael Rodriguez (Employee ID: IT-2903), to expedite resolution of a non-urgent system configuration task. This action violated multiple provisions of our Information Security Policy, specifically:

- Section 6.4: Access Control - "Access to information systems shall be granted based on business need, least privilege, and need-to-know"
- Acceptable Use Policy: Prohibition against sharing authentication credentials
- NIST SP 800-53 Control AC-2: Account Management requirements

INVESTIGATION FINDINGS:
Following detection by our privileged access monitoring system (CyberArk), I conducted an investigation which confirmed:

1. Mr. Thomas verbally provided his administrative password to Mr. Rodriguez at 10:42 AM
2. Mr. Rodriguez used these credentials to access the production server management console from 10:45 AM - 11:23 AM
3. No malicious activity occurred during this access period
4. Mr. Thomas stated the motivation was to help expedite a configuration change while he attended a scheduled meeting
5. Both employees acknowledged understanding this violated security policy

DISCIPLINARY ACTION TAKEN:
In accordance with our Progressive Disciplinary Policy (Three-Strike System), I issued Mr. Thomas a formal verbal reprimand on January 8, 2026, at 3:00 PM, which has now been documented as follows:

**FIRST STRIKE - FORMAL WRITTEN WARNING**

Action Taken:
- Formal counseling session conducted on January 8, 2026
- Reviewed Information Security Policy requirements and consequences
- Immediate mandatory password reset completed
- Required to complete refresher training on "Access Control and Credential Management" (Course ID: SEC-101) by January 17, 2026
- 30-day probationary monitoring period with enhanced access logging

Mr. Thomas acknowledged understanding of:
- The seriousness of the violation
- That this constitutes the first strike under our three-strike disciplinary policy
- That a second violation may result in suspension without pay and mandatory retraining
- That a third violation may result in termination of employment
- His obligation to report any future security concerns through appropriate channels

NOTIFICATION AND CORRECTIVE ACTIONS:
- Mr. Thomas's direct supervisor (IT Operations Manager - Jennifer Walsh) has been notified
- Enhanced monitoring of privileged access for both involved employees implemented for 90 days
- All IT Operations team members will receive mandatory refresher training on credential management by January 31, 2026
- Incident documented in Security Incident Log (Incident ID: SEC-2026-0012)

DOCUMENTATION FOR PERSONNEL FILE:
