This termination is for cause and constitutes the THIRD STRIKE under our Progressive Disciplinary Policy.

DISCIPLINARY HISTORY AND PREVIOUS WARNINGS:

Your employment has been subject to progressive disciplinary action due to repeated violations of information security policies:

**First Strike - January 10, 2026:**
- Violation: Sharing privileged administrative credentials with another employee
- Action: Formal written warning with mandatory retraining
- Documentation: DISC-2026-001-JT
- Warning Period: 24 months

**Second Strike - March 18, 2026:**
- Violations: Unauthorized access to financial database systems, improper extraction of confidential employee salary data, after-hours access without authorization, and failure to report security testing activities
- Action: Three-day suspension without pay, final written warning, revocation of privileged access, and placement on 90-day Performance Improvement Plan
- Documentation: DISC-2026-034-JT
- Status: Final warning with zero-tolerance for additional violations

**Third Strike - April 27, 2026 (TERMINATION OFFENSE):**

On April 27, 2026, at approximately 11:47 PM, you committed multiple serious violations of our Information Security Policy that directly violated the terms of your Performance Improvement Plan and the explicit access restrictions placed on your account following your March 18, 2026 final warning.

VIOLATIONS RESULTING IN TERMINATION:

1. **Attempted Unauthorized Access to Restricted Systems:**
   - At 11:47 PM on April 27, 2026, you attempted to access the production financial database server (FIN-DB-PROD-01) using a service account that you were explicitly prohibited from accessing
   - You made seven (7) failed login attempts using credentials belonging to a service account you discovered in an unsecured configuration file
   - This system was specifically identified in your March 18, 2026 access restriction list as off-limits

2. **Circumvention of Security Controls:**
   - You accessed the system from your personal laptop via VPN, rather than your assigned workstation which had enhanced monitoring
   - You disabled local audit logging on your workstation prior to the access attempts
   - These actions demonstrate deliberate intent to evade detection and monitoring

3. **Violation of After-Hours Access Policy:**
   - Access attempts occurred at 11:47 PM without required written authorization
   - You did not submit an after-hours access request as mandated by your March 18, 2026 restrictions
   - You were explicitly required to obtain approval 24 hours in advance for any after-hours access

4. **Violation of Performance Improvement Plan Terms:**
   - Your PIP explicitly required "zero security policy violations" during the 90-day period
   - You were on day 40 of your 90-day PIP at the time of this incident
   - This violation occurred during the intensive monitoring period

5. **Breach of Trust and Confidentiality:**
   - When confronted on April 28, 2026, you initially denied the access attempts
   - You later admitted to the attempts but claimed you were "just checking if the restrictions were really enforced"
   - Your actions demonstrate continued poor judgment and disregard for organizational policies despite two previous warnings

6. **Policy References Violated:**
   - Information Security Policy Section 6.4: Access Control
   - After-Hours Access Procedure (AH-001)
   - Performance Improvement Plan Terms and Conditions (PIP-2026-008-JT)
   - ISO 27001:2022 Control 6.4: Disciplinary Process
   - ISO 27001:2022 Control 8.2: Privileged Access Rights
   - NIST SP 800-53 Controls: AC-2, AU-6, AU-12

INVESTIGATION AND FINDINGS:

On April 28, 2026, Sarah Johnson (CISO) and the Digital Forensics team conducted an immediate investigation that conclusively determined:

- Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system logged all seven failed access attempts with timestamps, source IP addresses, and credentials used
- Forensic analysis of your assigned workstation revealed disabled audit logging services at 11:32 PM on April 27, 2026
- Network analysis confirmed VPN connection from your home IP address at 11:45 PM on April 27, 2026
- You accessed an unsecured configuration file on a development server at 9:23 PM on April 27, 2026, that contained service account credentials
- You did not report discovering unsecured credentials, which is a mandatory security reporting requirement
- No after-hours access request was submitted through the required approval system

When interviewed on April 28, 2026, at 9:15 AM, you acknowledged:
- Making the access attempts using discovered service account credentials
- Knowing the financial database system was explicitly restricted per your March 18, 2026 disciplinary action
- Understanding that you were required to report discovered credentials rather than attempt to use them
- Deliberately using your personal laptop to avoid workstation monitoring
- Failing to obtain required after-hours access authorization
- Recognizing that any policy violation during your PIP period would result in termination

DETERMINATION AND DECISION:

After careful review of the investigation findings, consultation with Sarah Johnson (CISO), your supervisor Jennifer Walsh (IT Operations Manager), Legal Department, and David Thompson (CTO), I have determined that your continued employment cannot be sustained for the following reasons:

1. **Pattern of Repeated Violations:** This is your third documented security policy violation in 108 days, demonstrating a persistent pattern of disregard for organizational policies and security controls.

2. **Escalating Severity:** Each violation has shown progressively worse judgment, from credential sharing, to unauthorized data access, to deliberate circumvention of security controls and monitoring systems.

3. **Violation During Final Warning Period:** This incident occurred while you were on a final written warning with explicit understanding that any additional violation would result in immediate termination.

4. **Breach of PIP Terms:** Your Performance Improvement Plan required zero policy violations, and you violated this requirement on day 40 of the 90-day period.

5. **Deliberate and Intentional Conduct:** The evidence demonstrates deliberate planning and intentional circumvention of security controls, including disabling audit logs and using personal equipment to evade monitoring.

6. **Failure to Learn from Previous Discipline:** Despite extensive retraining, counseling, suspension, and monitoring, you have failed to demonstrate the judgment and compliance required for your position.

7. **Breach of Trust:** Your position as Systems Administrator requires the highest level of trust and integrity. Your repeated violations have irreparably damaged that trust.

8. **Risk to Organization:** Continued employment poses unacceptable risk to organizational security, data protection, and compliance with ISO 27001:2022 and other regulatory requirements.
